About Jeep

Jeep is an American automobile brand that is a division of FCA US LLC (formerly Chrysler Group, LLC), a wholly-owned subsidiary of the Italian-American corporation Fiat Chrysler Automobiles. Jeep has been part of Chrysler since 1987, when Chrysler acquired the Jeep brand, along with remaining assets, from its previous owner American Motors Corporation (AMC).

History

Jeep's current product range consists solely of sport utility vehicles and off-road vehicles, but has also included pickup trucks in the past. The first Jeep brand vehicles were produced in 1941, making it the oldest off-road vehicle brand. The original Jeep was designed for military use during World War II, and it gained widespread popularity among the general public in the postwar years.

Models

  • Wrangler: A series of compact and mid-size (Wrangler Unlimited and Wrangler 2-door JL) four-wheel drive off-road SUVs.
  • Cherokee: A line of SUVs available in five-door and four-door models.
  • Grand Cherokee: A mid-size SUV that offers more space and luxury compared to the Cherokee.
  • Compass: A compact crossover introduced for the 2007 model year.
  • Renegade: A subcompact crossover SUV and the smallest model in Jeep's product range.
